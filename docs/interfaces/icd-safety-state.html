/safety/state

Unified safety state published by safety_monitor. The single source of truth for whether motion and tool engagement are permitted.

1.0

Purpose

The /safety/state topic is the unified status output of safety_monitor. It tells the rest of the system — including the mobile app, mission API, and system monitor — what safety state the platform is in, what triggered it, and whether motion and tool engagement are permitted.

It is the observational companion to the gating action at /hal/cmd_vel_safe. A consumer reading this topic can distinguish "rover stopped because operator released joystick" from "rover stopped because a person was detected."

4.0

Semantics

The reasons array is human-readable but stable: consumers may match strings to drive app icons and localization. Example values:

  • "person_in_danger_radius" — ESTOP, clearable after person leaves + 3 s dwell.
  • "tilt_exceeded" — ESTOP, clearable after rover levels + dwell.
  • "estop_button_pressed" — ESTOP, not clearable; requires /safety/reset.
  • "battery_critical" — CRITICAL, not clearable until charged.
  • "geofence_violated" — ESTOP, clearable when rover re-enters.
  • "topic_stale:/perception/people" — ESTOP, clearable when topic resumes.
  • "rogue_publisher:/hal/cmd_vel_safe" — ESTOP, not clearable until diagnosed.
  • "startup_incomplete" — ESTOP at boot until self-test completes.

Empty reasons array means state = OK with no active triggers.

Requirements

  • REQ-ICD-SAF-001-01
    Publication rate shall be ≥ 10 Hz at all times when safety_monitor is running, regardless of state.
    verified by: TEST-SAF-010
  • REQ-ICD-SAF-001-02
    State changes shall be published within 20 ms of the triggering condition crossing its threshold.
    verified by: TEST-SAF-011
  • REQ-ICD-SAF-001-03
    The motion_permitted and tool_permitted flags shall be false whenever state ≠ OK, with the single documented exception of CRITICAL allowing motion for safe stop.
    verified by: TEST-SAF-017
  • REQ-ICD-SAF-001-04
    Latched states shall report clearable=false; auto-clearable states shall report clearable=true.
    verified by: TEST-SAF-019

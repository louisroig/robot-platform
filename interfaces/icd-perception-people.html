1.0

Purpose

The /perception/people topic carries the subset of /perception/detections corresponding to living things — humans, dogs, cats, birds on the ground, wildlife. It is the primary safety input of its kind: the safety_monitor reacts to entries here with ESTOP or WARNING depending on distance.

Safety-critical interface

Correctness of filtering matters. A false negative (a person classified as an obstacle) defeats the perceptual safety layer. The living-thing filter is explicit and liberal — any COCO class that could plausibly be a living thing falls on this topic. See §4.

3.0

Message

Type: platform_msgs/msg/DetectionArray. Field schema identical to ICD-PER-001. Only detections whose class appears in the living-thing whitelist are included.

Whitelist (iteration 1): person, dog, cat, bird, horse, sheep, cow, bear. Deliberately liberal — a misclassification yielding "bear" when the truth is a large dog is harmless; the safety response is the same.

4.0

Semantics

An empty array means "no living things detected this cycle" — not "perception is silent." Silence is staleness, which the safety monitor treats as worst-case.

The filter preserves the original confidence and position. No additional filtering or smoothing is applied here; the safety monitor handles temporal smoothing (a person that disappears for one frame is still "present" until the clear-dwell elapses).

5.0

Timing

Same rate as upstream, target 10 Hz. Filter adds ≤ 2 ms latency.

6.0

QoS

RELIABLE, KEEP_LAST depth 2, VOLATILE. Deadline 150 ms.

Open Issues

  1. OPEN-01 — Small children sometimes misclassified as "backpack" or "couch" by YOLOv8n. Mitigation via minimum-size filter + confidence tuning. A specialized model for small-human is an iteration-2 consideration.
  2. OPEN-02 — Pets of unusual appearance (hairless breeds, reptiles) may not be in the whitelist. Current policy: any detection within 1 m triggers caution regardless of class (future).

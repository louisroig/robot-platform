Notes

Every requirement below is a hazard control. The safety case for iteration 1 rests on these; losing any one requires an explicit mitigation argument before fielding.

2.0

Requirements

  • SR-001
    The system shall provide a physical e-stop button that, when pressed, shall cut motor power within 100 ms without software involvement.
    Hardware path is the first and most trusted safety layer. Independent of any software fault.
    allocated to: e-stop relay in motor power rail · verified by: TEST-HAL-040 (software echo latency), hardware inspection
  • SR-002
    The system shall cease all motion within 500 ms of detecting a person within 3 meters.
    allocated to: yolo_detector, living_thing_filter, safety_monitor, roboclaw_driver · verified by: TEST-SAF-011
  • SR-003
    The system shall cease all motion within 500 ms of tilt exceeding 25 degrees.
    allocated to: imu_driver, safety_monitor · verified by: TEST-SAF-018
  • SR-004
    The system shall enforce geographic boundaries as defined by user-drawn polygons.
    allocated to: geofence_enforcer, safety_monitor · verified by: VRF-FIELD step 9
  • SR-005
    Safety-critical software shall operate independently of non-critical software (separate callback groups, separate threads).
    Prevents a stuck-non-critical callback from blocking safety reasoning.
    allocated to: safety_monitor, roboclaw_driver · verified by: TEST-SAF-025, TEST-HAL-009
  • SR-006
    Loss of any safety-relevant sensor input shall be treated as worst-case (fail-safe).
    Absence of data is unsafe, not safe. Staleness → ESTOP.
    allocated to: safety_monitor · verified by: TEST-SAF-014
  • SR-007
    Recovery from latched safety states shall require explicit operator action.
    Automatic un-latching could resume motion unexpectedly.
    allocated to: safety_monitor, mission_api · verified by: TEST-SAF-022
  • SR-008
    The system shall self-test on startup and shall not permit motion until all required inputs are confirmed active.
    allocated to: safety_monitor · verified by: TEST-SAF-020
  • SR-009
    The system shall detect and reject unauthorized publishers on safety-critical topics.
    Single-authority is architectural. Runtime detection is the current enforcement mechanism (SROS2 would be hard enforcement).
    allocated to: system_monitor, safety_monitor · verified by: TEST-SAF-016
  • SR-010
    Non-finite values (NaN, Inf) in motion commands shall trigger safe-halt.
    allocated to: roboclaw_driver, safety_monitor · verified by: TEST-HAL-011

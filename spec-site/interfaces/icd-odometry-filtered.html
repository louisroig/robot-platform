Local-frame EKF output fusing wheel odometry and IMU. Input to the global EKF.

1.0

Purpose

Output of the local EKF. Combines /hal/odom and /hal/imu/data into a drift-bounded pose in the odom frame. Used by the global EKF; not used directly by mission code.

2.0

RoleFuses with SLAM pose and optional GPS.
3.0

Message

Type: nav_msgs/msg/Odometry. Frame: odom, child: base_link. Fields mirror ICD-HAL-003 but with tighter covariance reflecting IMU-corrected yaw.

4.0

Semantics

The EKF fuses wheel odom x/y/yaw with IMU orientation and yaw rate. Wheel odom's linear pose and the IMU's yaw are the principal signals; the filter suppresses short-term wheel-slip-induced yaw error.

The odom frame drifts over time relative to map. This is correct — /odometry/filtered is the continuous, smooth, high-rate source; /localization/pose is the absolute, discontinuous-on-loop-closure source.

5.0

Timing

50 Hz ± 5 Hz.

6.0

QoS

RELIABLE, KEEP_LAST depth 10, VOLATILE.

7.0

Fault

If either input fails, ekf_local continues with the remaining input at increased covariance. If both fail, ekf_local ceases publication; ekf_global fails over to SLAM-only (still in map frame).

8.0

    verified by: TEST-LOC-010
  • REQ-ICD-LOC-002-02
    Yaw covariance shall decrease monotonically during 10 s of straight-line travel from startup, reflecting IMU bias convergence.
    verified by: TEST-LOC-011
